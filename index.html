1st Annual Rocky Mountain Fluid Mechanics Research Symposium

The Rocky Mountain Fluid Mechanics (RMFM) Research Symposium is a local technical meeting bringing together students, faculty, and professional researchers with a common interest in fluid mechanics. The primary purpose of the symposium is to provide student researchers along the Colorado Front Range and the greater Rocky Mountain Region the opportunity to present and discuss their work in a casual setting. Both oral and poster presentations will be hosted; however, neither is a prerequisite for meeting attendance. Beyond local networking, other professional development opportunities will be offered including a Resume/CV/Research Statement review table hosted by professors and research professionals. Please contact info@rockymountainfluids.org if you would like more information about RMFM2015.

Program

The symposium program will consist of a series of consecutive topical sessions in biological and environmental fluid mechanics, biofuels and reacting flows, colloidal systems, fundamentals, aerodynamics and wind energy, and numerical methods. Each topical session will consist of 14 minute oral presentations delivered by undergraduate and graduate students, as well as postdoctoral researchers. The symposium will take place in the Discovery Learning Center (DLC) at the University of Colorado, Boulder on August 4, 2015 from 8:00am to 5:00pm, with a keynote presentation by Dr.\ Diego Donzis from Texas A&M University 1pm and poster sessions during coffee breaks.

The technical program, including a schedule of events, can be downloaded here: RMFM 2015 Technical Program.

Abstracts for the keynote presentation and all of the oral and poster presentations can be downloaded here: RMFM 2015 Abstracts.
